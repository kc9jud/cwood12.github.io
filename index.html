Hi! I'm Charlotte

I am the Astronomy & Astrophysics Prize Postdoctoral Fellow at Iowa State University. I got my Ph.D. in Physics at the University of Notre Dame where I worked on supernova cosmology under Dr. Peter Garnavich. My research focuses on understanding type Ia supernovae progenitors and what effects the intrinsic variations in type Ia supernovae have on the Hubble constant measurement. Other research interests of mine include light echoes, white dwarfs, brown dwarfs, scientific writing/communication, and physics education.

Research & Publications

My primary research interests include type Ia supernovae progenitors, supernova cosmology, and light echoes. I work in optical & near-IR wavelengths, using both ground-based and HST imaging. Looking forward, I am most excited about the upcoming Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope.
